Research & Direction

Mobility in the Last One Mile

Life in Urban Space Re-urbanization is happening in America, people would like to live in the center of a city in the future, per capita living space will decrease while the need of living functions won’t, so those services and activities that improve life quality but less frequently be used will move towards cities rather than to families.

Public Sharing Transportation 5G is coming, IoT will be a common service in the future, let alone autonomous automobiles, while people will hardly have motivations to purchase private autonomous vehicles since no driving experience is needed. But people still need private space, so public sharing transportation system that provides relatively private space will be more possible in the future.

Better Mobility Lighter material, smaller and more efficient batteries will be available on automobiles in the future, people would get better mobility and can be taken to even the narrowest road in the city.

Focused Activities: Working and Studying Vehicle space is quite suitable for group working / studying scenario, especially for a future with a trend of working at home, the space is private, time promised and make you concentrated. Plus, sharing cars will enable people who don’t know each others to meet, they all have higher education, the vehicle space is just suitable for sharing experiences and knowledges.

Aim : To prevent the participants from focusing on less important issues in the following lab evaluation, I decided to have a heuristic evaluation to the implemented prototype 0 which contains back-end algorithm.

Recruitment : design experts with a learning background in DAAP, N=3

Steps : 

①

Individually find usability problems as much as possible

②

Group brainstorm : share problems and propose design ideas

③

Design representative talk with development team to list development priority

Online Session : Miro + WebEx

Data Analysis : Affinity grouping according to a criteria of seriousness

Design: prototype 1

Minor but Important Issues Fixed

The key findings are:

  • The gap between the design deliverable and the front-end implementation.
  • Pure technical issues. 
  • Neglecting general human reactions. 
  • Less consideration of using scenarios. 
  • Dilemma of introducing specialty of the IQS.

Final Decision : High priority: minor but important usability issues. Low priority: functions related to CIS
