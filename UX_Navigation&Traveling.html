DESIGN OPPORTUNITY

How might we design an App for Chinese GenZ to make their travel more "On the Road"?

I came up with this idea after an interview research method practice. At that time, I compared the navigation habits of 3 generations (how they find their ways from point A to B) in a Chinese family. I found that while all 3 generations have a tendency to navigate by proxy in unfamiliar locations, especially in traveling scenarios, the youngest generation, GenZ, is more likely to trust electronics' help. Besides, people are always more concerned about "how to get there", which runs counter to the meaning of traveling, "on the road".

SECONDARY RESEARCH

Target User Group: Chinese GenZ in tier 3 and 4 cities.

I went through a Popular Media Scan (101 Design Methods, P24) to have a look at what is happening and emerging in a broader topic: Chinese GenZ & traveling. By going through top Google Search results, TikTok, Weibo, RED, Mafengwo, WeChat Article... I've noticed that:

  • New trends in traveling: Chinese GenZ prefer self-guided travel more than ever
  • Self-guided travel service are not yet saturated and have the potential to grow
  • Since a large number of people (GenZ) are moving towards newer lifestyles driven by new media

Then the data were analyzed with open coding, axial coding, and selective coding. 

Since it is psychology-based research due to Baidu Companies' expectation, key findings from interviews are not presented as “insights” but rather as transcriptions listing users' needs in 4 low sense of control scenarios, which serves as a user need library. Since every user need is worth serving, we're only going to design several of them due to limited time. Finally, the raw material for the following brainstorm session was 4 groups of 5 user needs each based on different low sense of control scenarios, and they were randomly selected from the user need library.

INSIGHT

How do we Understand the User Needs ?

A brainstorming session was held to enlighten insights and solutions on the user needs listing above. Designers, researchers, and developers joined in the session. My role was the participant in the session. There are 3 rounds of activities in the brainstorm session, and all members getting involved were divided into 2 groups (5 members/group). 

Beginning with a brief overview of our research status and what we will be doing in this session, the Paper Passing activity aims to share the understanding of user needs from perspectives of each member, resulting in gathering personal insights from different group members around the same user need on each paper. Finally, 5 papers are collected and shared. Then the group voted to select those insights worth designing, using "I want" and "I need" as criteria.

These are the selected insights from the session:

 General Overtaking 

  • Users hope to understand why the autonomous driving is making such a decision perceptually in a natural way.
  • Users hope to know what autonomous driving is predicting and going to respond, retaining driver simple decision making opportunities.

Overtaking large vehicles 

  • Users hope to interact with large vehicles/ its drivers to make them know I'm going to take over.
  • Users hope to get rid of anxiety when inevitably having to follow large vehicles or drive side by side for a while if this decision is the best in specific situations.

Driving on unfamiliar roads

  • Users hope to be aware of the traffic rules of this place (e.g., speed limit) when driving on unfamiliar road to release their anxiety of illegal risk.
  • Users hope to reduce anxiety of waiting in traffic.

Parking

  • Users hope to control the autonomous driving vehicle as precise as possible, seeing evidence that autonomous driving vehicles are better than manual driving.
