problem articulation

How might we improve user's Sense of Control in Driving when they are using autonomous driving technology? 

A User Research was conducted, it breaks down the initial question into sub-questions that can be studied and answered through psychological modeling, translating the question "improve trust" into "improve one of the factors affecting trust: the sense of control in driving".

Finally, 4 driving scenarios with low sense of control are extracted. They are:

  • General overtaking
  • Overtaking large vehicles
  • Driving on unfamiliar roads
  • Parking

Then I conducted user interviews to explore the needs of users in each of these 4 scenarios.

interview

What are the Needs of Our Users in each of these 4 scenarios?

14 Participants were recruited (11 as drivers + 3 as passengers users), and each participant was interviewed after experiencing 4 simulated L4 autonomous driving scenarios. The interview collected data on their gestures, experiences, psychological feelings and motivations in the simulated scenario.

Then the data was analyzed with open coding, axial coding and selective coding. 

Since it is a psychology-based research due to Baidu Companies' expectation, the results of the interviews are not presented as “insights”, but rather as an enumeration of user needs for the 4 low sense of control scenarios, which serves as reference material for the brainstorm session. Since it was planned that the insights could be derived from the joint brainstorming of designers, researchers, and developers, the subject topics of brainstorm session were selected randomly from all of those user needs. The selected topics (user needs) are:

General Overtaking

  • I hope that the driving style of automatic driving can be similar with an experienced driver (e.g. low error rate, smooth driving, standardized driving actions, quick and decisive actions), making judgments about lane changes of passing vehicles, acting quickly and decisively.
  • I hope that autonomous driving can predict and respond to emergencies, retaining driver's control of the vehicle in emergencies.

Overtaking large vehicles 

  • I hope that the large vehicles know that I'm going to take over.
  • I hope that I can avoid following large vehicles and driving side by side, because I worry about falling cargo from large vehicles.

Driving on unfamiliar roads

  • When driving on unfamiliar roads, I need to know the rules of the road (e.g. speed limit) to ensure that I am not driving illegally
  • Reduce time perception while waiting in traffic

Parking

  • I'd like to get more precise position information when backing up, real-time feedback on position changes caused by the operation (I'd like the autopilot to be able to back up even on narrow roads)

Design

How might we ?

I chose one piece of those needs that I found most interesting to design, in order to improve the sense of control of that scenario.

Then the data was analyzed with open coding, axial coding and selective coding. 

The key insights are:

  • General overtaking
  • Overtaking large vehicles
  • Driving on unfamiliar roads
  • Parking
