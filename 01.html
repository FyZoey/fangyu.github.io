problem articulation

How might we improve user's Sense of Control in Driving when they are using autonomous driving technology? 

A User Research was conducted. It breaks down the initial question into sub-questions that can be studied and answered through psychological modeling, translating the question "improve trust" into "improve one of the factors affecting trust: the sense of control in driving".

Then I conducted user interviews to explore the needs of users in each of these 4 scenarios.

interview

What are the Needs of Our Users in each of these 4 scenarios?

14 Participants were recruited (11 as drivers + 3 as passengers users), and each participant was interviewed after experiencing 4 simulated L4 autonomous driving scenarios. The interview collected data on participants’ gestures, experiences, psychological feelings, and motivations in the simulated scenario.

Then the data were analyzed with open coding, axial coding, and selective coding. 

Since it is psychology-based research due to Baidu Companies' expectation, key findings from interviews are not presented as “insights” but rather as transcriptions listing users' needs in 4 low sense of control scenarios, which serves as a user need library. Since every user need is worth serving, we're only going to design several of them due to limited time. Finally, the raw material for the following brainstorm session was 4 groups of 5 user needs each based on different low sense of control scenarios, and they were randomly selected from the user need library.

Design

How might we Understand the User Needs and Provide Solutions?

A brainstorming session was held to enlighten insights and solutions on the user needs listing above. Designers, researchers, and developers joined in the session. My role was the participant in the session. There are 3 rounds of activities in the brainstorm session, and all members getting involved were divided into 2 groups (5 members/group). 

Beginning with a brief overview of our research status and what we will be doing in this session, the 1st activity aims to share the understanding of user needs from our perspective. The name of this activity is Paper Passing, resulting in gathering personal insights from different group members around the same user need on each paper. Finally, 5 papers are collected and shared. Then the group voted to select those insights worth designing, using "I want" and "I need" as criteria.

These are the selected insights (original user needs are on the left):

General Overtaking

  • I hope that the driving style of autonomous driving can be similar to an experienced driver, making expert judgments about lane changes of passing vehicles, acting quickly and decisively.
  • I hope that autonomous driving can predict and respond to emergencies, retaining a driver's control of the vehicle in emergencies.
  • Users hope to understand that autonomous driving is making a wise decision perceptually in a natural way.
  • Users hope to know what autonomous driving is
    predicting and potentially going to respond, retaining driver simple decision making
    opportunities.

Overtaking large vehicles 

  • I hope that the large vehicles know that I'm going to take over.
  • I hope that I can avoid following large vehicles and driving side by side because I worry about falling cargo from large vehicles.
  • Users hope to interact with large vehicles/ drivers with car-network and make them know I'm going to take over.
  • Users hope to get rid of anxiety when inevitably having to follow large vehicles or drive side by side for a while if this decision is the best in specific situations.

Driving on unfamiliar roads

  • I hope to know the rules of the road (e.g., speed limit) when driving on unfamiliar roads to ensure that I am not operating illegally.
  • I hope to reduce time perception while waiting in traffic.
  • When driving on unfamiliar roads, I need to know the rules of the road (e.g., speed limit) to ensure that I am not operating illegally
  • Reduce time perception while waiting in traffic

Parking

  • I hope to get more precise position information when backing up, real-time feedback on position changes caused by the operation (I'd like the autopilot to be able to back up even on narrow roads).
